Japanese Ginger Dressing

You probably recognize this dressing from a visit to a Japanese steakhouse. Despite its seemingly exotic nature, Japanese ginger dressing has a simple list of ingredients and is a cinch to make.

All you need are a few kitchen staples and a blender. This dressing pairs well with a simple salad of iceberg lettuce, chopped tomatoes, and grated carrot, but feel free to experiment. I personally enjoy it in a cabbage slaw or over sliced cucumbers with a sprinkle of sesame seeds .

INGREDIENTS

  • 7 ounces of peeled and chopped carrots
  • 4 ounces of peeled and chopped onion
  • 1 Tbsp of granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup of soy sauce
  • 1/2 cup of rice vinegar
  • 1/2 tsp of salt
  • 3/4 cup of vegetable oil

DIRECTIONS

  1. put all of the ingredients except the oil in a blender and puree
  2. slowly add the oil and blend until emulsified
  3. serve with your favorite salad
